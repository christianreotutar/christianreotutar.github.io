Electrostatics

Coulomb's Law

$\vec{F_{12}} = {1\over 4\pi \epsilon_0} {q_1q_2\over r_{12}^2} \hat{r}$

Gauss' Law

$\int\vec{E}\cdot d\vec{A} = {Q_{enclosed} \over \epsilon_0}$
$E = {1\over 4\pi \epsilon_0} {q\over r^2}$ (point charge)
$E = {\lambda\over 2\pi\epsilon_0r}$ (infinite line)
$E = {\sigma\over 2\epsilon_0}$ (infinite sheet)

Electric Potential Energy

$W_{AB} = {q_1q_2 \over 4\pi \epsilon_0} ({1\over r_A} - {1\over r_B})$
$\Delta U = -W$
$U_r = \Delta U_{\infty r} = {q_1q_2\over 4\pi \epsilon_0 r}$ (pair of charged particles)
$U_{System} = k \sum\limits_{pairs} {q_iq_j \over r_{ij}}$(system of N particles)
${\Delta U} = - \int_A^B{q\vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l}}$

Electric Potential

Electric Potential Difference

$\Delta V_{A\rightarrow B} = $V(B) - V(A)$ = {\Delta U_{A\rightarrow B} \over q} = - \int_A^B{\vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l}}$
$V(r) = k {Q\over r}$ (point charge)
$\vec{E} = -\vec{\triangledown}V$ (i.e. $E_x = - {\delta V\over \delta x}$)
$\vec{E} = {\vec{F}\over q}$

Conductors and Capacitance

$C = {Q \over V}$
$E = {Q \over \epsilon_0A} = {\sigma \over \epsilon_0} = {\Delta V \over d}$
$Q = \lambda L = \sigma A = \rho V$
$\Delta V_{A\rightarrow B} = -\int_A^B {\vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l}}$ (potential difference)

Potential Energy

$U = {1 \over 2}QV = {1 \over 2} {Q^2 \over C} = {1\over 2} {CV^2}$
$U = {Q^2\over 2C} = {1\over 2}C{\Delta V}^2$ (U stored in charged capacitor)
$u = {1\over 2} \epsilon_0 E^2$ (energy density)

Capacitors

$C = {\epsilon_0 A \over d}$
$C_{new} = \kappa C_{old}$
Dielectric medium: replace vacuum permittivity $\epsilon_0$ with $\kappa\epsilon_0$

Parallel

$V_{equiv} = V_1 = V_2$
$Q_{equiv} = Q_1 + Q_2$
$C_{equivalent} = C_1+C_2$

Series

$Q_{equiv} = Q_1 = Q_2$
$V_{equiv} = V_1 + V_2$
${1\over C_{equivalent}} = {1\over C_1}+{1\over C_2}$

Electric Current

$I = {dq\over dt}$ (unit: Ampere, C/s)
$J = {I\over A_{cross}}$
$I = \int{\vec{J}\cdot d\vec{A}}$ (electric current through cross section)
$J = n_{e}ev_{drift}$ ($n_e = N_{avogadro} {\rho_{mass} \over M}$, current density)
$J = \sigma E$ ($\sigma =$ conductivity)

Resistance

$R = {V \over I}$ (Ohms law)
$R = {1\over\sigma}{L\over A}$ (where ${1\over\sigma} = \rho =$ resistivity)

Parallel

$V_{equiv} = V_1 = V_2$
$I_{equiv} = I_1 + I_2$
${1\over R_{equivalent}} = {1\over R_1}+{1\over R_2}$

Series

$I_{equiv} = I_1 = I_2$
$V_{equiv} = V_1 + V_2$
$R_{equivalent} = R_1+R_2$

Power

$P = {dU \over dt} = IV$ (unit: Watt)
$P_{dissipated} = I^2R$

Kirchoff's Rules

$\Delta V_{loop} = 0$ for any closed loop
$\Sigma I_{in} = \Sigma I_{out}$ at a junction

Real Batteries

$V_b = V_0 ({{R \over r} \over {1+ {R \over r}}}) $

RC Circuits

$\tau = RC$

Charging Capacitor

$q(t) = CV_b(1-e^{-t \over \tau})$
$I(t) = {V_b \over R} e^{-t \over \tau}$

Discharging Capacitor

$q(t) = CV_be^{-t \over \tau} = q_0e^{-t \over \tau}$
$I(t) = -{V_b \over R} e^{-t \over \tau} = -{q_0 \over \tau} e^{-t \over \tau}$

Power

$P_R(t) = RT^2e^{-2t \over \tau}$
$P_C(t) = [{q_0 \over c} (1-e^{-t \over \tau})] I_0 e^{-t \over \tau}$
$P_{Batt}(t) = P_R(t) + P_C(t) = V_b I_0 e^{-t \over \tau}$

Magnetism

Lorentz Force

$\vec{F} = q (E + \vec{v} \times B)$
$R = {mv \over qB}$ (Radius in a magnetic field)

Centripetal Motion

$\vec{F}_{net} = m \vec{a} $
$a_c = {v^2 \over r}$
$k = {1 \over 2}mv^2$

Forces/Torques on Currents

Force

$\vec{F_{wire}} = I\vec{L} \times \vec{B}$ (I carrying wire in B field)
$F_{loop} = 0$ (without Torque)

Torque

recall $\tau = \vec{r}\times\vec{F}$
$\mu = NI\vec{A}$ (magnetic dipole moment)
Use RH rule to find direction of $\vec{A}$: fingers in dir of I
$\tau = IABsin\theta = \vec{\mu} \times \vec{B}$

Potential Energy

$\int_{\theta_1}^{\theta_2} \tau = \int_{\theta_1}^{\theta_2} (-\mu B sin\theta) d\theta$
$\Delta U = - \vec{\mu} \cdot \vec{B}$

Biot Savart

$\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7}$

Magnetic Field

$d\vec{B} = {\mu_0 I \over 4\pi r^2} d\vec{s} \times \hat{r}$
$d\vec{s}$ in dir of $I$ and $\hat{r}$ from $d\vec{s}$ to $P$
$B = {\mu_0 I \over 2 \pi R} $ (current carrying wire)
$R$ is perpindicular distance to $P$
Use RH to find dir of $\vec{B}$: thumb in dir of $I$
Use $d\vec{B}$ to solve $B$

Ampere's Law

$\int\limits_{loop} \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I_{enclosed}$

Inside of a Wire

$B = {\mu_0 I r \over 2\pi a^2}$ for r < a, radius of wire
$B = {\mu_0 I \over 2 \pi r}$ for r > a

Infinite Sheet of Current

$B = {{1\over 2}n I \mu_0}$

Motional EMF

$E = vB$

Conducting Bar

$\varepsilon_{conducting bar} = vBL$ (potential difference)
$I_{conducting bar} = {vBL \over R}$ (from Ohm's Law)
$P_{conducting bar} = {(VBL)^2 \over R}$

Other Shapes

$\varepsilon_{wire loop} = vL(B_{bottom} - B_{top})$
$\varepsilon_{generator} = \omega A_{loop}Bcos(\omega t)$

Faraday's Law

$\Phi_B = \int\vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} $
$\varepsilon_{induced} = -{d\Phi_B \over dt}$
$\int\vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l} = -{d\Phi_B \over dt} = {d\over dt}\int\vec{B}\cdot d\vec{A}$

Lenz' Law

$\varepsilon_{induced}$ opposes $d\Phi_B \over dt$

Induction & RL Circuits

$ L = {\Phi_B \over I}$ (units: Henry(H) = T-m^2/A)
$\varepsilon = -L{dI\over dt}$
$ B = \mu_0nI $
$ \Phi_B = \mu_0n^2z\pi r^2I $
$ L = {\Phi_B \over I} = \mu_0n^2z\pi r^2 $

RL Circuits

$ I_L(t) = {V_b\over R} (1-e^{-t\over\tau}) $
$ \tau = {R \over L} $
$ V_L = V_be^{-Rt\over L} = V_be^{-t\over\tau} $

Battery Opened

$ I(t) = {V_b\over R} e^{-t\over\tau} $
$ \tau = {L\over R_{eq}} $
$ V_L(t) = -2V_be^{-t\over\tau} $

Energy

$ U = {1\over2}LI^2 $
$ u_B = {B^2\over 2\mu_0} $

LC/RLC Circuits

$ q(t) = q_0cos(\omega_0t) $
$ I = -{d\omega\over dt} = q_0\omega_0sin(\omega_0t) $

Energy

$ U_C = {q^2\over 2C} = {q_0^2\over 2C}cos^2(\omega t) $
$ U_L = {Lq_0^2\omega_0^2\over 2}sin^2(\omega t) = {q_0^2\over 2C}sin^2(\omega t) $
$ total = U_C + U_L = {q_0^2\over 2C} $
$ \omega_0 = {1\over \sqrt{LC}} $

LCR Circuits

$ \alpha = {R\over 2L} $
$ \omega^2 = {1\over LC} - {R^2\over 4L^2} $
$ q(t) = q_0e^{-\alpha t}cos(\omega t) $

AC Circuits

Generator + Resistor

$I_R = {\epsilon_msin(\omega t) \over R}$

Generator + Capacitor

$X_C = {1\over\omega C}$ (reactance)
$Q_C = CV = C\epsilon_msin(\omega t)$
$I_C = {dQ\over dt} = \omega C \epsilon_mcos(\omega t)$
$I_C = {\epsilon_mcos(\omega t) \over X_C}$

Generator + Inductor

$X_L = {\omega L}$ (reactance)
${dI_L\over dt} = {\epsilon_msin(\omega t) \over L}$
$I_L = {-E_mcos(\omega t) \over X_L}$

Phasors

Inductor leads Resistor/Current leads Capacitor by 90
$Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} (impedence) $
$\tan{\phi} = {X_L-X_C \over R} $
$I_m = {\epsilon_m \over \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2}} $

Resonance and Power

Resonance

When $I_m$ is a maximum
$\omega = \omega_0$
$\omega_0 = {1\over \sqrt{LC}}$
Z is minimized and $X_C = X_L$
$\phi = 0$

Max Current

$I_m = {\epsilon_m\over Z} = {\epsilon_m\cos{\phi}\over R}$
$X = {\omega\over\omega_0}$
$Q^2 = {L\over R^2C} $
$I_m = {\epsilon_m\over R} {1\over \sqrt{1 + {Q^2(X^2-1)^2\over X^2}}} $

Power

$\lt P_{generator}\gt = \lt P_{resistor}\gt = {1\over2}I_m^2E_m \cos{\phi}$
$\lt P_{inductor}\gt = \lt P_{capacitor}\gt = 0 $
$\lt P_{generator}\gt = {\epsilon_{rms}^2\over R} {X^2\over X^2 + Q^2(X^2-1)^2} $

Root Mean Square

$\epsilon_{rms} = {\epsilon_m\over\sqrt{2}} $
$I_{rms} = {I_m\over\sqrt{2}} $

Q Factor

$Q = 2\pi [{U_{max}\over\Delta U}] $
$Q^2 = {L\over R^2C}$ for driven LCR only
$V_{L_{max}} = V_{C_{max}} = Q\epsilon_m$ at resonance only

Ideal Transformer

$V_s = {N_s\over N_p} V_p$
$I_p = {N_s\over N_p} I_s$

Displacement Current & EM Waves

$\int{\vec{B}\cdot d\vec{l}} = \mu_0(I_{enclosed} + I_{displaced})$
$I_{displaced} = \epsilon_0 {d\Phi_t\over dt} $
